About the Book

The story governments did not want told.

In the years following the September 11, 2001 attacks, the United States established military commissions at Guantanamo Bay to try individuals it classified as enemy combatants. The legal architecture of those commissions was designed to produce convictions — not to deliver justice.

Major Michael Mori was assigned as defense counsel to David Hicks, an Australian national held at Guantanamo for years without charge and without meaningful legal process. What he witnessed — and what he chose to do about it — is documented in this book.

This is not simply the story of one man's detention. It is a rigorous, unflinching account of what happens when democratic governments abandon the principles they claim to uphold, and of the few individuals who refused to look away.

About the Author

Major Michael Mori

Military Lawyer · Guantanamo Defense Counsel · Author

Major Michael Mori served as a military defense counsel and was assigned to represent David Hicks — one of the first individuals prosecuted before the military commission system established at Guantanamo Bay following the September 11 attacks.

His work on the Hicks case brought him into direct conflict with an institution that had both the power and the incentive to silence dissent. He pursued the case anyway. In The Company of Cowards is his account of what that meant — legally, professionally, and personally.
